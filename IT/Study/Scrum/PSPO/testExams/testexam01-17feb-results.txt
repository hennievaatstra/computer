Question 1:
What should Developers do if the Product Owner is unavailable?
:A.  Within the Sprint, the Developers make the best decisions possible to assure progress toward the Sprint Goal, re-aligning with the Product Owner once they are available again.

Question 2:
When does a Developer become accountable for the value of a Product Backlog item selected for the Sprint?
:C.  Never. The entire Scrum Team is accountable for creating value every Sprint.

:E.  They collaborate often so the Product Owner can make informed decisions in balancing effort and value of Product Backlog items.

Question 5:

Question 6:
The Developers find out during the Sprint that they are not likely to build everything they forecast. What would you expect a Product Owner to do?
:C.  Re-negotiate the selected Product Backlog items with the Developers.

Question 7:

:False

Question 8:
What best describes the relationship of the Product Owner and the stakeholders?
:C.  The Product Owner actively asks for stakeholder input and expectations to incorporate into the Product Backlog.

Question 9:
What is a Product Backlog?
:D.  The Product Backlog is an emergent, ordered list of what is needed to improve the product.

Question 10:
When can the Product Backlog be updated?
:B.  At any time when done by the Product Owner or a delegate.

Question 11:

Question 12:
